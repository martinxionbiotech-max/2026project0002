Calculator

How to Use

  1. Select the vehicle type and wrap scenario.
  2. Choose full or partial coverage (roof, hood, doors, or custom).
  3. Input roll width, waste factor, unit price, and roll length.
  4. Click Calculate Now to get area, rolls, and cost estimates.

Tip: Add 5% extra safety stock when ordering film to avoid color-lot mismatch delays.

Calculation Method

The tool uses average exterior area by vehicle class, applies your selected coverage ratio and waste factor, then converts total area into required roll length and material budget.

  • Effective area = Base area × Coverage ratio × Scenario factor × (1 + Waste factor)
  • Required roll length = Effective area ÷ Roll width
  • Roll count = Round up (Required roll length ÷ Roll length per roll)
  • Material cost = Effective area × Unit price

Application Scenarios

  • Wrap shops: produce quick and consistent customer quotations.
  • Ad teams: estimate material demand for fleet branding projects.
  • PPF installers: pre-plan stock and labor scheduling.
  • Car owners: compare budget options before placing orders.

Limitations

  • Results are estimation values, not laser-accurate panel templates.
  • Body kits, spoilers, and custom aero parts increase actual consumption.
  • Film stretch characteristics vary by brand and product line.
  • Installer experience and process quality strongly affect waste rates.
